Olga Slivko

Assistant Professor in Information Systems
Rotterdam School of Management, Erasmus University
Research Teaching CV

I study digital platforms (user-generated content and social networks) and how platform governance affects knowledge generation and diffusion. I also examine how online participation relates to broader economic and societal processes such as economic conditions and migration.

My research uses data from platforms including Wikipedia, Twitter, and Microworkers.

My work is published in Information Systems Research, MIS Quarterly, Journal of Economics & Management Strategy, and Experimental Economics.

Research interests

  • Digital platforms and platform governance
  • Knowledge production and diffusion on UGC platforms
  • Social networks and information diffusion
  • Online labor markets and economic outcomes
  • Migration and knowledge flows

Background

Before joining RSM, I was a Postdoctoral fellow in Digital Economy at ZEW (Mannheim, Germany) from 2012 to 2020 and taught “Digital Platforms and Markets” at Darmstadt University of Applied Sciences.

I received my PhD in Economics from Universitat Rovira i Virgili (Spain).
